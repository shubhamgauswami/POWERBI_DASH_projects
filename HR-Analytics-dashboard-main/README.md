# HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

## Introduction
This project presents an **HR Analytics Dashboard** built using **Power BI** to analyze employee data and understand workforce trends, attrition patterns, and key HR metrics. The dashboard helps organizations identify the main factors contributing to employee turnover and supports **data-driven HR decision-making** to improve employee retention and workforce planning.

---

## Dataset Details
The dataset used in this project includes employee-level HR information with the following key attributes:

- **Employee ID** – Unique identifier for each employee  
- **Age** – Employee age  
- **Gender** – Male or Female  
- **Education** – Education background  
- **Job Role** – Employee job position  
- **Department** – Department name  
- **Salary** – Monthly income of employees  
- **Years at Company** – Employee tenure  
- **Attrition** – Whether the employee left the company  

---

## Objectives
The main objectives of this dashboard are:

1. Measure the **overall employee attrition rate**
2. Identify **job roles and departments with high attrition**
3. Analyze attrition based on **salary, age, and experience**
4. Understand **employee demographics and workforce distribution**
5. Provide an **interactive dashboard** for HR decision-making

---

## Dashboard Overview
The Power BI dashboard includes:

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Total number of employees  
- Total attrition count  
- Attrition rate (%)  
- Average employee age  
- Average salary  
- Average years at company  

### Visualizations
- **Bar chart:** Attrition by job role  
- **Bar chart:** Attrition by salary slab  
- **Column chart:** Attrition by age group  
- **Pie chart:** Attrition by education  
- **Trend chart:** Attrition by years at company  

### Interactive Features
- Filters to analyze attrition by **department, role, and demographics**
- Dynamic visuals for deeper workforce insights  

---

## Key Insights 

1. **High Attrition in Specific Job Roles**
   - Laboratory Technicians, Sales Executives, and Research Scientists show higher attrition.

2. **Salary Impact on Attrition**
   - Employees in lower salary brackets have higher attrition rates.

3. **Age Group Trends**
   - Most attrition occurs in the **26–35 age group**, indicating early-career turnover.

4. **Experience Level Impact**
   - Employees with fewer years at the company are more likely to leave.

5. **Department-Level Insights**
   - Certain departments experience higher turnover, requiring focused retention strategies.

---

## Conclusion
The HR Analytics Dashboard provides valuable insights into **employee attrition, workforce demographics, and salary trends**. Using Power BI, the dashboard enables HR teams to:

- Identify high-risk attrition areas  
- Improve employee retention strategies  
- Support workforce planning  
- Make data-driven HR decisions
